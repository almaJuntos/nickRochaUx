My Road Into UX

Biology/Psychology

I studied biology and psychology because I'm an analytical thinker who values the scientific method (Who, What, When, Where, and Why). I now can use UX research techniques when solving physical and behavioral health problems.

Social Work

I identified people's needs through interviews, assessments, and documentation. I use empathy to meet people where they are which further developed services and programs that meet service users goals, continued use, and ongoing use in the future.

Front-End Web Development

My intro in front-end web development merged design and development. I now can communicate and understand different problem solving methods when creating design systems that are collaborative and efficient.

UX Design

I moved into UX design because I want to further my social science background and create tech products that are used everyday. I believe that people who are closest to the problem are closest to the solution when improving products that maintain need, use, value, and adaptation.

My Design Process

A good design is a regenerative process between users and stakeholders. It is not created in an executive conference room based on one brilliant idea. Design is a non-linear process that uses active communication and collaboration between each team member. My goal as a UX designer is to advocate for users' needs when defining organizational goals for products or services.
